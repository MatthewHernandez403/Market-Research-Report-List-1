<p><p>The orthopedic braces and support devices market is categorized into three main types: upper-limb orthoses, lower-limb orthoses, and spinal orthoses. Upper-limb orthoses are designed to support the arm, wrist, and hand, often used for fractures or post-surgery recovery. Lower-limb orthoses focus on the knee, ankle, and foot, aiding in mobility and stability for conditions like sprains or post-operative rehabilitation. Spinal orthoses provide support to the back and neck, assisting in alignment and pain relief for various spinal disorders and injuries.</p></p>
