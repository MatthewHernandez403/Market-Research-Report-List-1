<p><p>The non-wearable sleep monitoring devices market is categorized into contact and contactless types. Contact devices involve physically interacting with the user, such as under-mattress sensors that assess sleep patterns by measuring movement and pressure. In contrast, contactless devices monitor sleep without any physical touch, utilizing technologies like infrared, radar, or audio analysis to track breathing and movement. Both types aim to enhance sleep quality and offer insights into sleep disorders while providing comfort through non-intrusive methods.</p></p>
