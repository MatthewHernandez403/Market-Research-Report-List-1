<p><ul><li>Assets Backed Securities (ABS)</li><li>Collateralized Debt Obligations (CDO)</li><li>Mortgage-Backed Securities (MBS)</li></ul></p>
<p><p>Structured finance encompasses various financial instruments designed to improve capital efficiency and manage risks. Asset-Backed Securities (ABS) are created by pooling various types of loans or receivables, such as auto loans or credit card debt, and then issuing securities backed by these assets. Collateralized Debt Obligations (CDOs) pool different debt instruments, including ABS and corporate bonds, to create tranches with varying risks. Mortgage-Backed Securities (MBS) are specifically backed by mortgage loans, providing investors exposure to real estate credit risk.</p></p>
