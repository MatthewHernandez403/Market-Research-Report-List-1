<p><ul><li>Cholinesterase Inhibitors</li><li>NMDA Antagonists</li></ul></p>
<p><p>The Alzheimer's Disease drugs market primarily features two types: cholinesterase inhibitors and NMDA antagonists. Cholinesterase inhibitors, such as donepezil and rivastigmine, enhance communication between nerve cells by increasing acetylcholine levels, improving cognitive function. NMDA antagonists, like memantine, help regulate glutamate activity, protecting nerve cells from excessive stimulation and improving memory and cognitive symptoms. Both drug categories aim to manage symptoms and enhance quality of life for patients, reflecting a growing focus on addressing Alzheimer's disease progression.</p></p>
