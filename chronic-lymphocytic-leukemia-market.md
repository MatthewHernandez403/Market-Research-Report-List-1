<p><p>The chronic lymphocytic leukemia (CLL) market is a segment of the broader leukemia market, which includes several types of the disease. Acute myeloid leukemia (AML) is characterized by rapid progression and affects myeloid cells. Chronic myeloid leukemia (CML) is a slower-progressing disorder arising from myeloid progenitor cells. Acute lymphocytic leukemia (ALL) involves sudden proliferation of lymphoblasts and primarily affects children. In contrast, CLL is a slow-growing lymphoproliferative disorder that predominantly affects older adults, leading to various treatment options and market dynamics.</p></p>
