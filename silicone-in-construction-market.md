<p><p>Silicone in construction encompasses various types, including fluids, elastomers, resins, and gels. Silicone fluids serve as sealants and lubricants, enhancing water resistance and flexibility. Elastomers are used for their rubber-like properties in joints and gaskets, providing durability and movement accommodation. Resins are utilized for coatings and adhesives, offering excellent adhesion and chemical resistance. Gels, known for their cushioning and thermal insulation, are applied in specific applications where flexibility and shock absorption are critical. Together, these silicone types enhance performance and longevity in construction projects.</p></p>
