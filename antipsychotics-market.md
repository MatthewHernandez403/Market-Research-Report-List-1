<p><p>The antipsychotics market is categorized into three main types: First Generation, Second Generation, and Third Generation. First Generation antipsychotics, or typical antipsychotics, primarily target dopamine receptors but may cause significant side effects. Second Generation antipsychotics are atypical, offering a broader mechanism of action with reduced side effects, improving treatment adherence. Third Generation antipsychotics focus on stabilizing dopamine levels, minimizing adverse effects further, and enhancing cognitive function. Each generation addresses different patient needs and treatment outcomes in managing psychiatric disorders.</p></p>
